Frequently asked questions

Dead simple answers for first‑time semi‑private flyers.

  • Book‑by‑the‑seat on small private aircraft. You use private terminals (FBOs), skip long lines, and share the cabin with a few other passengers.
  • Charter = you rent the whole aircraft. First class = big airline cabin. Semi‑private = you buy a seat on a small private plane that has a set schedule or frequent shuttles.
  • Usually private terminals (FBOs) near major cities—less walking, fewer crowds. We’ll show the FBO name when you request seats.
  • Bags are limited due to aircraft size. Typical allowance: one small carry‑on + personal item; larger items may require advance approval. Tell us what you’ll bring and we’ll match policies.
  • Many semi‑private operators allow pets with limits (size/kennel/fee). Toggle “Pets” in the search and we’ll only surface pet‑friendly options.
  • Pricing varies by route and season. Use a budget per seat in the form or tap “Explore deals.” We’ll also notify you when prices drop on a route you track.
  • Submit the form to request seats. We route your request to an operator/broker and send you a link to complete booking directly with them.
  • You’ll still need government ID. Screening is at the FBO and is typically faster than commercial terminals.
  • Turn on “Flexible dates” in the form and we’ll search ±3 days and set a price alert for that window.
  • Create an alert from the form or deals page. We’ll email/SMS when seats appear or prices change on your route.
